MIT Blueprint

Accepted to attend the MIT Blueprint hackathon on campus at MIT.

Got into MIT Blueprint and spent the day on campus at MIT. Sat in on a class with Professor Srinivasan Raghuraman and got exposed to ideas at the intersection of computer science and game theory — how incentive structures and algorithmic thinking apply to real system design. The environment was genuinely stimulating: being around other curious people building things in a compressed timeframe is a different kind of learning.

What I built there: A.C.R.E. (GreenhouseAgent) — a sensor, actuator, and recommendation stack for greenhouse automation using Arduino, FastAPI, OpenAI, and a local Random Forest model, with Supabase cloud logging and a live web dashboard.

GitHub Devpost

YC x Google DeepMind Gemma Voice Agents Hackathon

A day with YC partners, founders, and builders learning about voice AI and on-device models.

Attended the YC x Google DeepMind Gemma Voice Agents Hackathon. Got to hear directly from a YC General Partner, which gave a sharp perspective on what separates ideas that compound from ones that stay demos. Spent time talking with founders and builders across the event, and had real conversations with the Cactus founders about how they think about on-device inference and developer ergonomics for local AI. The energy of being in a room full of people actually shipping things, not just talking about them, was something you can't replicate online.

What I built there: YConstruction — a voice-first iPhone app for construction defect reporting with on-device AI and BIM-grounded issue sync, using SwiftUI, Gemma 3n, Whisper, and Supabase, with a Blender/Bonsai integration for architect-side review.
